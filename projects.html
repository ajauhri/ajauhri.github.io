Connections of Evolvability to Correlational SQ Learning [report] [slides]

This works describes under certain restrictions that evolvability is equivalent to the Probably Approximately Correct (PAC) model. The report elaborates the model of evolvability to correlational statistical query model. The motivation behind this work was to study computational guarantees of learning using evolutionary operators like mutation and recombination since evolutionary algorithms do not have guarantees of convergence in general.

Comparative Study of Nearest Neighbour Techniques [report] [src]

Nearest neighbor(NN) are relevant for numerous machine learning applications. Here performance and accuracy results of NN algorithms for dense and sparse datasets are compared. In this work, NN algorithms include locality sensitive hashing and cover trees due to previously studied speed guarantees. Also, a toy problem to fill missing patches of an image using locality sensitive hashing is demonstrated.

rkt-heaps [report] [src]

Description and performance results of binomial and fibonacci heaps are elaborated. Graphs show how actual run-time performance corresponds to bounds as stated in theory. Also, a comparison is done with an existing library of binary heaps.

Recommendations using Coupled Matrix Factorization (with Vagelis Papalexakis) [report]

We formulate the problem of recommendation with additional information as an instance of Coupled Matrix Factorization with two different types of regularization, namely $l_1$ and $l_2$ norms. We also experiment on two diverse, real world applications(Netflix and Stackoverflow) and demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach in terms of rating prediction.

Measuring Client Device Behavior on the Modern Web (with Erik Reed) [report] [src]

We hypothesize that most of the client devices such as mobile phones and laptops have workload which require fetching of state information from a remote server when browsing the internet. Understanding patterns of accessing state information is essential to perform optimizations. In this work, we collect and analyze patterns of network usage and system calls for over a thousand popular websites.
